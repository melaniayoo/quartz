---
title: Zero Trust Model
---
Zero Trust Model is a cybersecurity model based on the idea of “Never trust, always verify.”

In traditional security, users inside the company network were often trusted. However, Zero Trust does not automatically trust anyone, even if they are inside the network.

The main principles are:
* Never Trust, Always Verify
    * Every user and device must be verified before access is allowed.
* Least Privilege Access
    * Users only get the minimum access they need.
* Continuous Monitoring
    * User activity is constantly checked, even after login.

For example, even if an employee logs into a company system, Zero Trust may still check their password, MFA, device status, location, and access permissions.

In short, Zero Trust means every access request must be verified instead of automatically trusted.

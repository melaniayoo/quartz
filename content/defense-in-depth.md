---
title: Defense in Depth
---
Defense in Depth is a cybersecurity strategy that uses multiple layers of protection.

The idea is that if one security layer fails, other layers can still protect the system.

# Examples of Security Layers
* Firewall
    * Blocks suspicious network traffic.
* Access Control
    * Limits who can access systems and data.
* MFA
    * Adds extra verification during login.
* Encryption
    * Protects data even if it is stolen.
* Monitoring
    * Detects suspicious activity.
* Backups
    * Helps recover data after an incident.

# Simple Example
A company may use a firewall, strong passwords, MFA, antivirus software, and backups together.
Even if an attacker bypasses one layer, they still have to get through the others.
